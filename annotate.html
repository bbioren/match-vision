Write the audio-description commentary

What we need:
  • Write commentary that can be spoken by a voice AI during or after the clip. It should sound natural, energetic, and useful, not like dry labels.
  • Prioritize blind/low-vision access: ball location, direction of play, player spacing, pressure, danger, and why the moment matters.
  • Describe only what is visible in the clip. If the clip ends before the outcome, say that instead of guessing.
  • If you do not know player names, use shirt numbers when visible. Otherwise use positions or roles: left back, right winger, goalkeeper, nearest defender, player in white, etc.
  • Use 2-4 concise sentences. Make it vivid and enthusiastic, but keep it factual enough for downstream voice AI.

Experts do not submit examples. Select a “To do” clip to write new commentary.

Watch the clip more than once if needed. Your goal is to give a fan who cannot see the screen the same visual understanding and excitement a sighted fan gets.
