## Guidelines for Your Commentaries

### Structure (aim for 2-4 sentences)
1. **What's happening** (action + ball location)
2. **Who's involved** (shirt color, position, or player name if visible)
3. **Where it matters** (field zone, defensive/attacking third)
4. **Why it's interesting** (crowd reaction, danger level, tactical significance)

### Tone
- **Enthusiastic** (match the energy of the moment)
- **Factual** (no guessing, no inventing outcomes)
- **Spatial** (use directions: left, right, far post, near post, top of the box, etc.)
- **Specific** (numbers, positions: "left back," "right winger," not generic "the team")

### Player Identification
- If you see a **shirt number**: use it ("the #9 with the ball")
- If you see a **position/role**: describe it ("the left back in blue")
- If you know a **player name**: use it confidently
- **Never invent** a player name you can't confirm from the footage

### When Action Doesn't Complete
- If a shot ends before the save: "The ball is heading for goal..." (don't say if it scores)
- If a foul is called mid-play: "The referee has whistled..." (don't explain why)
- If the clip cuts off: Be honest: "...as the play continues out of frame"

### Examples of Good Commentaries

**Good**: "England's right winger plays a low cross into the six-yard box. Croatia's defender slides in to clear, but the ball ricochets toward the near post where an England striker waits to pounce. The crowd roars as he strikes it!"

**Good**: "A loose ball near the center circle. England's midfielder intercepts and drives forward. Croatia's defense retreats, opening space on the left flank. The left winger accelerates into the gap."

**Not good**: "England scores a beautiful goal." (You don't know the outcome until the shot is taken)

**Not good**: "The player in white" (use the number or position)

## Scoring: 7-Point Rubric

You'll rate each clip on these dimensions (1-7 scale):

1. **Factual Accuracy** (1-7)
   - Does the commentary match what's visible?
   - No invented details or false assumptions?

2. **Spatial Clarity** (1-7)
   - Can a blind listener visualize the field zones?
   - Left/right, near/far, tactical areas named?

3. **Player/Team Identification** (1-7)
   - Clear who has the ball and what color shirt?
   - Numbered or positioned players, not vague?

4. **Moment Significance** (1-7)
   - Does it explain *why* this matters tactically?
   - Danger level, crowd energy, or key moment noted?

5. **Tone/Enthusiasm** (1-7)
   - Does it match the intensity of the moment?
   - Passive clips sound calm, dangerous moves sound urgent?

6. **Completeness** (1-7)
   - Does it use the full 10 seconds of footage?
   - No rushing through important details?

7. **Hallucination-Free** (1-7)
   - Zero invented outcomes, players, or events?
   - Honest about what you actually see?
