How It Works

  1. Grant Camera Access: Click "Enable Eye Tracking" and allow camera permission
  2. Initial Calibration: WebGazer detects your eyes (no manual setup required)
  3. Watch the Video: Look at any part of the video you want to focus on
  4. Automatic Zoom: The video automatically zooms and pans to your gaze point
  5. Manual Override: Use Zoom In/Out buttons if you need different zoom levels

Use Cases

  • Low-Vision Sports Fans: Focus on a specific area of the field (e.g., the ball or a particular player)
  • Coach Review: Automatically focus on key tactical areas while reviewing footage
  • Highlight Capture: Let eye tracking guide you to the most interesting parts of a game
  • Accessibility Feature: Combine with audio description for complete sports accessibility

Technology

This demo uses WebGazer.js, a browser-based eye tracking library from Brown University. It runs entirely in your browser—no server, no cloud, no data collection.
