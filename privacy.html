MatchVision Eye Tracker — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-21

MatchVision Eye Tracker ("the Extension") is an accessibility tool that gives low-vision users gaze-controlled video zoom/pan, plus a voice assistant for hands-free control.

What the Extension processes

  • Webcam video. Used entirely in your browser to estimate where you're looking (via WebGazer.js). Webcam frames are never uploaded, stored, or sent to any server — gaze estimation happens locally on your device.
  • Microphone audio. When you use the voice assistant, your spoken audio is sent to Deepgram for speech-to-text, the resulting text is sent to Anthropic (Claude) to generate a response, and the response may be converted back to speech via Deepgram. See Deepgram's and Anthropic's own privacy policies for how they handle data sent to their APIs.
  • Page content. The Extension reads the structure of the page you're viewing to locate video elements and can control video playback/fullscreen state. It does not collect or transmit page content elsewhere.

What the Extension does not do

  • We do not sell or share your data with advertisers.
  • We do not persist voice conversation history beyond your current browsing session — it lives in memory and clears when you stop the voice assistant or close the tab.
  • We do not track your browsing activity beyond what's needed to operate gaze tracking and voice control on the page you're actively using the Extension on.

Permissions, explained

  • Camera / microphone — required for gaze tracking and voice interaction.
  • Host access (all sites) — the Extension works on any page with video, so it needs to be able to run wherever you choose to use it.
  • debugger — used briefly, only when you ask the voice assistant to make the video fullscreen, to grant the page the same fullscreen permission a real click would. It is not used to inspect, log, or transmit any other browsing activity.
